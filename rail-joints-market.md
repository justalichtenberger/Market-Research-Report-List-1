<p><p>The rail joints market comprises three primary types: standard rail joints, compromise rail joints, and insulated rail joints. Standard rail joints connect rail sections for continuity, ensuring structural integrity. Compromise rail joints are designed to accommodate different rail sizes, providing a seamless transition between varying rail profiles. Insulated rail joints facilitate electrical isolation between tracks, essential for signaling and safety systems. Each type serves specific functions within the rail infrastructure, improving efficiency, safety, and operational performance.</p></p>
