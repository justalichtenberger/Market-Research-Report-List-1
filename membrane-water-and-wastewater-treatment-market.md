<p><p>The membrane water and wastewater treatment market includes several key technologies that enhance water purification processes. Microfiltration employs porous membranes to remove suspended solids and bacteria, while ultrafiltration offers finer filtration, targeting larger viruses and colloids. Nanofiltration bridges the gap between ultrafiltration and reverse osmosis, removing smaller organic molecules and divalent ions. Reverse osmosis utilizes semi-permeable membranes to eliminate nearly all dissolved salts and contaminants, making it the most effective method for producing high-purity water. Each technology serves distinct applications based on specific treatment needs.</p></p>
